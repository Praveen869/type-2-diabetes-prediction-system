Awareness

Understanding Diabetes

Diabetes affects over 537 million adults worldwide. Early detection is the key to better outcomes and a healthier life.

Type 1 Diabetes

An autoimmune condition where the body stops producing insulin. Usually diagnosed early in life.

Type 2 Diabetes

The most common form. The body doesn't use insulin properly. Strongly linked to lifestyle and genetics.

Gestational Diabetes

Develops during pregnancy and usually resolves after birth, but increases future type 2 risk.

High Blood Glucose

Excess sugar in the blood damages vessels and nerves over time.

Risk Factors

Obesity, inactivity, family history, and age are key risk factors.

Prevention

Healthy diet, exercise, and regular screenings dramatically reduce risk.

Management

With proper care, people with diabetes can live full, healthy lives.
